What's involved in taking part

Is this study right for you?

You'll need all three of these to take part:

Living in rural or regional Australia
Parenting at least one child aged 0–12
Currently co-parenting with someone who can take part in the study with you. This person can be a partner, ex-partner, grandparent, or other important person in the life of your child(ren).

Click below to see more information about each step of participating.

Express interest ~2 min

Press next on this page, give your contact details, and answer a few quick questions — including when works best to talk. There's no commitment at this stage.

Speak to the research team ~10 min

A team member talks you through the project, asks questions to check your eligibility, and collects details of the co-parent you'd like to take part with.

If you're not eligible: the team will talk through other support options with you, so the conversation is still worthwhile.

The team has this same conversation separately with your co-parent, to check it's a fit for them too.

Read and sign consent ~5 min

If you're eligible, we email you a link to read and sign the consent form digitally. Sign only when you're comfortable — and contact the team any time with questions.

Your co-parent completes their own consent form too.

First online survey ~20–30 min

You'll answer questions about your family, mental health, life circumstances and relationships.

Why we ask so much: these answers help us understand what's happening for you, and let us compare before and after the support to see what changes. The detail is the point — it's how the study measures whether the support helps.

$20 gift card as thanks for completing it, sent by email.

Your co-parent completes the same survey.

Randomisation by chance

Your family is randomly allocated to one of the two supports below. It's done completely by chance — like flipping a coin — and a researcher will let you know which one by phone or email.

You can't choose your support: neither you nor the team decides — it's random. Both supports are genuinely valuable, and whichever you receive makes an essential contribution to the study.

Allocated by chance to one of these two paths:

Family Life Check In

A 30-minute phone call with a health professional to talk through your life and the supports you might need. One or both of you can take the call.

Family Foundations

Eight one-hour sessions, usually weekly, delivered online with a health professional. You and your co-parent attend together.

These run within 11 weeks of randomisation. If you can't commit to that, please tell the team before enrolling.

Both paths then continue to the follow-up surveys

Follow-up surveys ~15–80 min

There are two more survey points after your support:

About 12 weeks after randomisation: a survey with questions similar to the first, plus a short feedback survey about the support you received (~15–20 min).

Around six months in: a final survey with similar questions (~20–30 min). You'll also be invited to an optional 30-minute interview about your experience.

$20 gift card for each survey — plus another $20 if you take part in the optional interview.
