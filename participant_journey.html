What's involved in taking part

Is this study right for you?

To take part, participants must be:

Living in rural or regional Australia
Parenting at least one child aged 0–12
Currently co-parenting with at least one other person who can take part in the study with you. This person can be a partner, ex-partner, grandparent, or another important person in the life of your child(ren).

Express interest ~2 min

Press next on this page, provide your contact details and let us know when suits to speak on the phone about the project and next steps.

Discuss eligibility and consent to take part ~15 min

A team member will call you talk you through the project, ask some brief questions to check your eligibility, and will collect the details of the co-parent(s) you'd like to take part with (~10 min).

If you're eligible, we email you a link to read and sign the consent form digitally (~5 min).

A team member has the same conversation seperately with your co-parent(s), and they complete their own consent form.

First online survey ~20–30 min

You'll answer questions about your family, mental health, life circumstances and relationships.

Why we ask so much: these answers help us understand what's happening for you, and let us compare before and after the support to see what changes.

$20 gift card as thanks for completing it, sent by email.

Your co-parent completes the same survey.

Randomisation by chance

Your family is randomly allocated to one of the two supports below. This is done completely by chance, like flipping a coin. A team member will let you know the outcome by phone or email.

Why it's random: participants and researchers are not able to control which support a family receives. This ensures both groups have a mix of all kinds of people so that we can properly compare the two types of support.

Allocated by chance to one of these two supports:

Family Life Check In

A 30-minute phone call with a health professional to discuss family wellbeing and relationships, and identify any supports needed. One or both of you can receive this support.

Family Foundations

Eight one-hour sessions, usually weekly, delivered online with a health professional. You and your co-parent(s) attend together.

These run within 11 weeks of randomisation. If you can't commit to that, please tell the team before enrolling.

Follow-up surveys ~15–80 min

There are two more survey points after your support:

About 12 weeks after randomisation: a survey with questions similar to the first, plus a short feedback survey about the support you received (~15–20 min).

Around six months in: a final survey with similar questions (~20–30 min). You'll also be invited to take part in an optional 30-minute interview about your experience.

$20 gift card as thanks, for completing each survey and the optional interview.

Your co-parent completes their own surveys and optional interview.
